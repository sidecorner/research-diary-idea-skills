# Idea Evaluation Criteria — Diary & Journaling Apps

## What Makes a Good Idea

An idea is worth pursuing when it:

- Addresses a **repeatedly mentioned** frustration — not a one-off complaint
- Fits the "light and forgiving" philosophy — low friction, no guilt design
- Can be scoped to a small team (1–3 people)
- Has a clear user action: write, track, reflect, review, share, etc.

Discard ideas that rely on a single data point or a personal anecdote with no corroboration.

## Signal Strength by Source Combination

| Sources with Signal | Strength |
|---------------------|----------|
| Reddit + HN + Qiita | Strongest — cross-platform, cross-language validation |
| Reddit + HN | Strong — English-speaking developer/power-user market |
| Reddit + Qiita | Strong — Japan-market focus with community validation |
| Single source only | Weak — treat as hypothesis, not validated pain point |

Ideas appearing in all three sources — especially with both English and Japanese interest — are the strongest candidates for Japan-market-focused products.

## Reliable Source Signals

### Reddit
- Posts where users describe **giving up** on journaling apps or habits
- Flairs: "Help", "Question", "Rant", "Advice Needed"
- Language patterns: "I wish it had X", "Why doesn't any app do Y", "I stopped because..."
- Recurring themes across **multiple subreddits** (cross-community = strong unmet need)

### Hacker News
- Show HN posts about journaling/diary tools with 10+ points or 5+ comments
- Ask HN threads about personal writing habits
- Discussions of gaps in tools like Obsidian, Logseq, Day One
- Threads about local-first or privacy-first note apps

### Qiita
- Personal projects developers built because **existing apps didn't fit**
- Articles about habit-formation challenges around journaling
- UX complaints from Japanese users (simplicity, privacy, handwriting feel)

## Market Landscape Context

The diary/journaling space is **crowded at the top** (Notion, Day One, Obsidian) but has many underserved niches:

- ADHD / neurodivergent users needing low-cognitive-load UX
- Non-English speakers wanting Japanese-first design
- People who have "tried and failed" repeatedly (no-guilt philosophy)
- Users who want zero cloud sync (local-first, privacy-first)
- Users who want AI that *reflects with* them — not writes for them

**Flag as competitive risk** any idea that overlaps with well-funded startups in the space.

**Revenue note**: Day One ($35/yr), Notion ($8–16/mo) prove willingness to pay. Free alternatives (Apple Notes, Google Keep) create pricing pressure. Differentiation through AI features, handwriting, premium templates, therapy-adjacent features, or team features tends to unlock payment.

---

## Privacy and Ethical Guidelines

- When quoting user posts, **paraphrase** — do not reproduce verbatim to avoid privacy concerns.
- Do not claim therapeutic efficacy without evidence.
- Avoid ideas that require storing sensitive mental health data without clear consent flows.
