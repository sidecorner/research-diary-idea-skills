# Idea Scoring Rubric — Diary & Journaling Apps

Each idea is scored across 5 dimensions, each on a 1–5 scale.
The goal is honest calibration, not inflated optimism. A "3" is a perfectly viable starting point.

---

## 1. 実現可能性 (Feasibility)

Can this be built with reasonable effort and available technology?

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 5 | Fully achievable with existing tools/APIs, no research required |
| 4 | Achievable with some integration work or learning curve |
| 3 | Achievable but requires specialized knowledge (ML, NLP, data analysis) |
| 2 | Major technical hurdles — custom models, privacy compliance, complex data |
| 1 | Currently not technically feasible or requires breakthrough research |

**Factors to consider**: local-first storage complexity, AI/LLM API integration, cross-platform sync, data export/import, privacy architecture, offline functionality requirements

---

## 2. 開発期間 (Development Time — Solo/Small Team)

How long would an MVP take for a solo developer or 2-person team?

| Score | Meaning | Timeframe |
|-------|---------|-----------|
| 5 | Very fast — can ship MVP in under 4 weeks | < 1 month |
| 4 | Fast — MVP in 1–2 months | 1–2 months |
| 3 | Medium — MVP in 2–4 months | 2–4 months |
| 2 | Slow — MVP in 4–6 months | 4–6 months |
| 1 | Very slow — requires 6+ months to reach anything shippable | 6+ months |

**Calibration**: Assume a capable solo full-stack developer with standard cloud tools.
Content-heavy products and complex sync/offline architectures take significantly longer.

---

## 3. 収益性 (Revenue Potential)

Can this generate meaningful revenue within 12–18 months of launch?

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 5 | Clear monetization — users demonstrably pay for diary/journaling tools (e.g., Day One, Notion) |
| 4 | Good pathway — premium features, one-time purchase, or niche subscription model |
| 3 | Viable but requires scale — freemium with conversion pressure, or affiliate |
| 2 | Difficult monetization — users expect free, or market is very small |
| 1 | Very hard to monetize — unclear who pays and why |

**Diary market note**: The journaling app market has proven willingness to pay (Day One at $35/year, Notion at $8–16/month). However, free alternatives (Apple Notes, Google Keep) create pricing pressure. Differentiation through: AI features, handwriting, premium templates, therapy-adjacent features, or team features tends to unlock payment.

---

## 4. 競合優位性 (Competitive Advantage / Blue Ocean)

How crowded is this space? Is there a defensible angle?

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 5 | Clear gap — no direct competitors, or all competitors are failing this niche |
| 4 | Some competition but obvious differentiation (ADHD-friendly UX, Japanese market, offline-first) |
| 3 | Competitive space but beatable with better UX or niche focus |
| 2 | Crowded — well-funded direct competitors (Notion, Day One, Obsidian) already here |
| 1 | Red ocean — incumbents dominate and switching cost is low |

**Key differentiation opportunities in diary/journaling**:
- ADHD/neurodivergent-friendly design (low cognitive load, no streaks, forgiveness)
- Japanese-language-first with local-first privacy
- Low-pressure / "no guilt" journaling philosophy
- AI reflection partner (not AI writing for you)
- Specific use cases: travel diary, parenting log, therapy homework, team retrospectives

---

## 5. 小規模開発適性 (Small Team / Solo Suitability)

How well is this idea suited to being built and run by a small team (1–3 people)?

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 5 | Perfect indie/solo project — can launch, grow, and sustain with 1 person |
| 4 | Manageable with 1–2 people, minimal ongoing operations burden |
| 3 | Doable but will feel stretched — needs some automation or part-time help |
| 2 | Requires dedicated team — content, support, or partnerships can't be solo'd |
| 1 | Fundamentally unsuited to small teams — logistics, regulatory, or operational complexity |

**Factors that hurt small team score**: real-time collaboration, enterprise sales, medical claims (therapeutic efficacy), physical/hardware components, large content editorial requirements.

**Factors that help**: SaaS/digital-only, self-serve onboarding, markdown/plain-text storage, community-driven content, no sync required (local-first), subscription model with low churn.

## Composite Score Interpretation

| Total (out of 25) | Interpretation |
|-------------------|---------------|
| 22–25 | Exceptional — prioritize immediately |
| 18–21 | Strong — worth prototyping |
| 13–17 | Viable — proceed with awareness of weak dimensions |
| 8–12 | Challenging — needs a key insight to unlock |
| 5–7 | Not recommended — fundamental barriers |
